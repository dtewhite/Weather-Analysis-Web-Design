Humidity vs. Latitude

The next factor we analyzed was how humidity varied further from the equator. We had very few expectations regarding this variable, but were none-the-less surprised by the results.

It appears based in this dataset that northern cities are more humid than those closer to the equator or in the southern hemisphere. This is a little strange as the specific dataset used was assembled while it was winter in the northern hemisphere and cold air tends to be dryer. The trend, however is undoubtedly evident.
